The Artist

Sonia Delaunay and the Birth of Modern Colour

Sonia Delaunay transformed colour into a living language. Her paintings, textiles, fashion, stage costumes and graphic design shared one philosophy: colour should create movement, emotion and rhythm rather than simply describe objects.

A Revolutionary Vision

Working alongside the avant-garde of Paris, Sonia Delaunay developed an artistic approach where circles, geometry and contrasting colours generated visual energy. Her work became one of the foundations of modern abstract art.

Unlike many artists of her generation, she refused to separate fine art from applied design. Paintings, clothing, books, interiors and architecture all became part of a single artistic universe connected by colour.

Innovation

Experimentation was central to her practice. Every composition explored balance, contrast, repetition and rhythm while remaining visually elegant and timeless.

Early Years

Academic studies and artistic education established the foundation for a lifetime dedicated to experimentation.

Simultaneism

The development of simultaneous colour relationships became the defining characteristic of Sonia Delaunay's artistic language.

International Recognition

Her influence expanded across Europe through exhibitions, fashion, illustration, theatre and publishing.

Lasting Legacy

Today her work continues to inspire artists, museums, designers and scholars around the world.
